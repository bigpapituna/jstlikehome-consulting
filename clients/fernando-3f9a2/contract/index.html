01

Engagement and scope

The Client engages the Consultant to build and manage multi-channel distribution for the Client's short-term rental portfolio (23 active listings in the Dominican Republic). The work includes:

  • Migration of the portfolio from Hostex to Hostaway, working with the Client's staff.
  • Creation, launch and optimization of listings on Booking.com, Expedia (direct account) and Hopper. Any channel beyond these three is a separate add-on, quoted and agreed in writing (see Section 03).
  • Optimization of existing Airbnb and VRBO listings: content, SEO, settings and pricing strategy.
  • Dynamic pricing with owner-minimum floors on every listing.
  • Payment configuration review (Stripe / PayPal) and fraud guardrails.
  • Automations: guest messaging, lock codes, owner statements and the guest portal.
  • Enrollment and ongoing management of channel visibility programs and campaigns.
  • Rebuild of the Client's website and creation of a new direct booking engine through Hostaway, with ongoing maintenance and optimization.
  • Progress calls with the Client every two weeks (10–15 minutes) and running updates in the shared group chat. The Consultant works day to day with the Client's operations staff (Alondra Infante).
Expedia is included only if a direct Expedia connection can be set up separately from VRBO in the Dominican Republic. Because Expedia and VRBO belong to the same group, a separate Expedia connection is not available in every market. If it is not available in the Dominican Republic, Expedia is treated as out of scope and the parties may agree on a replacement channel; the fees are unchanged.

Client responsibilities

  • Timely access to all accounts, listings, photos and property information.
  • Staff assistance for listing creation and migration tasks.
  • Keep Hostex active until the migration is complete (target: August 31, 2026).
  • Provide a list of current software subscriptions.
  • Respond to the Consultant's questions, approval and access requests within 36 hours.
Milestone dates in this agreement extend day-for-day for delays caused by the Client's side, including delayed access, materials or staff availability.

04

The January 31 performance gate

The gate — the Client's exit protection

The gate is met if both (a) and (b) are true:

(a) Deliverables. The Hostaway migration is complete and the portfolio's listings are live on the new channels in scope under Section 01 (Booking.com and Hopper, plus Expedia or an agreed replacement where a separate Expedia connection is available) with dynamic pricing and automations on, by September 1, 2026 (as extended for Client-side delays under Section 01).

(b) Outcome floor. By January 31, 2027, either: (i) at least 15% of the portfolio's Commissionable Revenue from reservations confirmed between August 1, 2026 and January 31, 2027 originates from channels other than Airbnb and VRBO; or (ii) at least 20 confirmed reservations have originated on the new channels. Meeting either one is enough. The outcome floor is also deemed met if the Client materially deviates from, or declines to implement, the Consultant's documented pricing or campaign recommendations during the measurement window.

Measurement. All numbers are taken from Hostaway reporting, which both parties can access and which is the single source of truth for this agreement.

If the gate is not met

  • The Client may terminate this agreement without any termination fee by written notice (email suffices) delivered between February 1 and February 7, 2027.
  • The Client keeps all work product: listings, channel accounts, configurations, automations and content. There is no recapture of the implementation discount.
  • If the Client terminates under this section, any full managed handover requested within 60 days of that termination is provided at no charge — the $2,500 clean-transition fee in Section 05 does not apply.
  • The option-month payments (February–May) are not owed.
  • Commission on reservations confirmed before termination remains payable as those stays complete.

If the gate is met

  • The agreement simply continues through its term, and the option-month payments become due as scheduled.
  • The implementation-discount recapture in Section 05 becomes applicable from this point on.
If the Client does not deliver written notice by February 7, 2027, the agreement continues in effect under its normal terms.
05

Term, renewal and termination

  1. Term. Twelve (12) months from the Effective Date, renewing automatically for successive 12-month terms unless either party declines renewal with 60 days' written notice before a term ends.
  2. Early exit. Other than the January 31 gate (Section 04), this agreement may end early only by mutual written agreement or for material breach that remains uncured 15 days after written notice describing it.
  3. On any end of this agreement. The Client owns and keeps its listings, channel accounts, guest data, content and configurations. The Consultant provides reasonable transition assistance and hands over credentials within 14 days.
  4. Reservation tail. Commission applies to reservations confirmed on or before the end date of this agreement, and is paid as those stays complete. The Consultant is paid on the pipeline built during the term, nothing beyond it. Until that commission is fully settled, the Client maintains the Consultant's read-only access to Hostaway reporting or provides the equivalent monthly statements.
  5. Implementation recapture (only if the January 31 gate is met). The implementation is valued at $34,500: the Client pays $9,000 in installments plus the $4,000 in option months, and the remaining $21,500 is recovered over time through commission paid under this agreement. If this agreement ends for any reason — including the Client declining renewal, or the Consultant terminating for the Client's uncured material breach — before cumulative commission totals $21,500, the Client pays the difference, so the implementation never costs more than its $34,500 list price. There is no time limit: once cumulative commission reaches $21,500, the balance is settled and nothing further is owed. Example: if $10,000 of commission has been collected when the agreement ends, $11,500 remains due. This recapture does not apply if the January 31 gate is not met, where the Client terminates for the Consultant's uncured material breach, or where the Consultant ends or declines to renew this agreement other than for the Client's uncured material breach.
  6. Clean transition. A full managed handover of listings, guest data and configurations is available on request as a one-time service ($2,500), so the Client's operation is never held hostage. The basic credential handover in this section remains included at no charge. Where the Client terminates under Section 04 for a missed January 31 gate, this managed handover is provided at no charge if requested within 60 days of that termination.
  7. Hostaway after exit. The Client may migrate the Hostaway subscription to its own direct account (retail rates apply), or continue under the Consultant's partner account at the same rate by mutual agreement.

General terms

  1. Independent contractor. The Consultant is an independent contractor, not an employee, partner or agent of the Client.
  2. Confidentiality. Each party keeps the other's non-public business information confidential and uses it only for this engagement.
  3. Ownership. The Client owns its listings, accounts, guest data and content, including work product created under this agreement once paid for. The Consultant may describe anonymized results for marketing; naming the Client requires the Client's written permission.
  4. Liability. Neither party is liable for indirect or consequential damages. Each party's total liability under this agreement is capped at the fees paid or payable to the Consultant in the 12 months before the claim.
  5. Indemnity. The Client indemnifies the Consultant against third-party claims arising from the properties and guest stays, from Client-provided content and materials (including photo and content rights), or from the Client's own operations, except to the extent caused by the Consultant's negligence or breach of this agreement.
  6. Force majeure. Neither party is responsible for delays caused by events beyond its reasonable control, including platform outages and natural events.
  7. Governing law and disputes. This agreement is governed by the laws of the Province of Ontario, Canada. The parties will first try in good faith to resolve any dispute directly within 30 days. Any dispute not resolved that way is finally settled by binding arbitration before a single arbitrator under the rules of the ADR Institute of Canada, seated in Ottawa, Ontario and conducted in English; judgment on the award may be entered in any court with jurisdiction.
  8. Notices. Formal notices under this agreement (including the Section 04 termination notice) are sent by email — to the Consultant at roy.y@jstlikehome.com, and to the Client at the address it uses on the signature page — or to another address a party later designates in writing. A notice is effective on the day it is sent unless the sender receives a delivery-failure message.
  9. Language. This agreement is executed in English. The Spanish rendering on this page is for convenience only; the English text governs.
  10. Entire agreement; amendments. This is the entire agreement and replaces prior proposals and discussions. Changes must be in writing signed by both parties; a signed email exchange or e-signature amendment suffices. This agreement may be executed electronically and in counterparts, which together form one agreement.
