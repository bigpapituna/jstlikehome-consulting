05

The Plan: Go Multi-Channel

This is where the biggest gains are. Listing across Booking.com, Expedia, and Hopper (alongside Airbnb) can more than double your visibility and removes single-channel risk.

One thing we'll get right from day one: each channel's price needs to bake in OTA commission, any promotional discounts, and local DR taxes, so your net stays where you want it and your owner-minimum floors hold on every channel.

06

Smarter Pricing: Dynamic Rates, Owner Floors Protected

You set rates by hand today, essentially guesswork, which leaves money on the table in peak weeks and empty nights in slow ones. The fix is dynamic pricing: rates that move with real market demand, seasonality, local events, and last-minute gaps, so each night is priced to what the market will pay.

The key safeguard is a floor under every listing. We set an owner-minimum price per unit that the system won't drop below, so dynamic pricing only works in your favor: it pushes rates up when demand is there and holds the line at the owner's minimum when it isn't. You get the upside of market-driven pricing without giving up control of the bottom.

A Couple of Decisions to Make Together

Branding vs. platform detection. A consistent name and light brand identity helps guests recognize and trust your inventory, and helps repeat guests find you. The tradeoff is that a strong brand "fingerprint" can make it easier for platforms to cluster your units. I'd recommend a measured approach: consistent quality and naming without over-exposing the operation. This is a strategic call for us to make together.

Expedia in the DR. Your VRBO listings route through Expedia, but that gives you no campaign or visibility boost the way a dedicated Expedia account would. We'll confirm with Expedia whether their Dominican Republic footprint allows a separate direct account.
