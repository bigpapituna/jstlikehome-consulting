01

Where we stand

Following the audit I prepared and our recent call, this proposal lays out how I would take Caribbean Dream Properties from its Airbnb-concentrated setup to a diversified, managed distribution operation. It also gives you two ways to structure the partnership so my incentives and yours point the same way.

The headline from the audit: your portfolio is under-distributed and under-optimized today. Read that as the opportunity, not a criticism. You already have a strong base of 23 listings, a 4.8+ rating, Superhost status, and a Booking.com award. Almost all of your volume still runs through a single channel. From here, the direction is up.

02

The opportunity

The market is moving in your favor

2025 was the strongest tourism year in Dominican history: 11.6 million visitors, with December alone setting a monthly record near 960,000 air arrivals. The United States drives about 39% of arrivals and Canada about 19%. Punta Cana handles around 62% of all air arrivals, which puts your east-coast units at the mouth of the funnel.

Demand is seasonal, and that seasonality is the core of what I manage for you: a strong peak from mid-December through March and April, a secondary peak in July and August, and softer shoulder months in May–June and September–October. Guests in your markets book 30–90 days ahead in peak season, so getting your channels live before September is what captures the December–March window.

Where you are leaving money on the table

  • Channel concentration. Running close to 100% through Airbnb leaves an estimated 75% of online travel-agency (OTA) viewership untapped: Booking.com, Expedia, and Hopper, where a large share of luxury and concierge travelers search.
  • No campaign visibility. Even where your listings reach Expedia via VRBO, you are not enrolled in the marketable campaigns and visibility programs (Booking.com Genius, Preferred Partner; Expedia's equivalents) that drive the bulk of OTA bookings.
  • Static pricing. Owner-fixed nightly rates with no market data leave revenue on the table on peak dates. A floor-price approach respects owner minimums while capturing upside.
  • Unused ancillary revenue. Early check-in, late checkout, cleaning, pet fees, and concierge add-ons can become a standalone revenue stream that helps offset platform costs.

Your portfolio is built to capture far more than it does today. The market just posted its best year on record, and you reach a fraction of it through one channel. The gap isn't demand. It's distribution, and distribution is the part I fix.

03

Action plan

The work runs in three phases. The first two get you live and optimized. The third is the ongoing management that compounds results over time.

Phase 1 · Stabilize

  • Resolve the technical issues from the audit: suspended or inconsistent listings, booking-site 404 errors, and calendar and verification gaps.
  • Review your Stripe and PayPal setup, confirm processor and payment configuration, and establish fraud guardrails (pre-authorizations and an automated risk-analysis charge) to cut chargebacks and fake bookings.
  • Confirm the migration path from Hostex to Hostaway for premium API connectivity.

Phase 2 · Build and launch

  • Stand up Hostaway: channel connections, automated messaging, owner statements, lock-code generation, and the guest portal.
  • Branding, SEO, and listing optimization: titles, descriptions, and a consistent naming convention so guests recognize your portfolio across channels.
  • Create and optimize OTA listings on Booking.com, Expedia (direct account), and Hopper, and enroll in their visibility programs and campaigns.
  • Implement dynamic / floor-price pricing through a specialist tool (PriceLabs or Wheelhouse), and switch on payment automation and upsells. These tools carry their own onboarding and subscription cost, available to you at a discounted rate through me.

Phase 3 · Optimize and manage

  • Active pricing management and campaign management across all channels as seasons and demand shift.
  • Quarterly performance reviews against the data, learning your true low and high seasons and tuning to them.
  • Adding new channels and programs as they emerge, and building a direct-booking / past-guest marketing base over time.
04

Setting expectations

You will notice I am not putting projected revenue figures in this proposal. That is deliberate, and it is part of how I work.

Several of your units price at $7,000+ per night, and today they sit almost entirely on one channel with little exposure. Nobody, including me, yet has reliable demand data at those price points. Putting a specific peak-season or annual number in front of you before your listings are live across channels and producing booking data would be a guess dressed up as a forecast. I would rather not start our relationship that way.

The direction is up. The size we learn together, once the channels are live and the data starts coming in.

What I can tell you: the demand is there at the market level and your distribution is not. That is a fixable problem. Rather than sell you a number I can't yet stand behind, I'd build the channels, let bookings come in, and set targets together from data we both trust.

Engagement terms

These are the commercial terms. They are fair to both sides, and they let you leave cleanly if it ever stops working. We formalize them in a short services agreement.

Term and renewal

  • Initial term: 12 months from launch, then auto-renewing in successive 12-month terms.
  • Either of us may decline renewal with 60 days' written notice before a term ends.
  • Performance assurance. If your new channels are not live and producing bookings by month 9, you may exit then with no termination fee. I'm confident enough in the work to stand behind it.

Dissolution of partnership

  • Reservation tail (Option A only). My commission applies to reservations confirmed on or before the end date, paid as those stays complete. I'm paid on the pipeline I built, nothing beyond it. Option B has no commission, so nothing carries over.
  • Implementation recapture (Option A only). Your setup is worth $34,500. You pay $9,000, and the remaining $25,500 is recovered over time through the 4% commission. If you end the partnership before that commission totals $25,500, you pay the difference, so the setup never costs more than its $34,500 list price. There's no time limit: once the 4% has brought in $25,500, the balance is settled and nothing is owed. Example: if I've collected $10,000 in commission when you leave, $15,500 remains due. Option B is paid at full rate, so nothing applies.
  • Clean transition. A full handover of listings, guest data, and configurations is available as a one-time service ($2,500), so there is never a situation where your operation is held hostage.
